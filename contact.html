The Council is in its early formation phase. The Hiroshima AI Assurance Protocol is under development at Draft v0.1. The Logistics Profile Phase 1 PoC has been completed with 株式会社オンザリンクス.

01
Who should join

Any domain where AI decisions
require verifiable conditions.

The Council is not limited to logistics or any single sector. It is designed for any organization or individual engaged in AI adoption where conditions, evidence, responsibility, and reviewability matter.

◎
Companies in high-accountability sectors
Where AI decisions affect safety, regulatory compliance, or significant operational outcomes.
◎
Technology developers and AI system providers
Building systems where adoption-state management and evidence-based verification are required by clients or regulation.
◎
Research institutions and universities
Working on AI governance, formal verification, accountability, or responsible AI deployment.
◎
Public-sector organizations
Developing AI policy, procurement criteria, or operational AI assurance frameworks.
◎
Individual engineers and researchers
Interested in contributing to open specification development, formal methods, or field validation work.

02
What participation involves

Contributing to an open
implementation standard.

01
Specification development

Contribute to the Hiroshima AI Assurance Protocol specification — message types, state models, evidence requirements, and domain profiles. The current focus is Draft v0.1.

02
Field validation

Participate in implementation PoC projects in your domain. The Logistics Profile Phase 1 is complete; Phase 2 and new sector profiles are open for validation partners.

03
Vocabulary and glossary

Help maintain the shared vocabulary based on the Responsibility OS glossary, and contribute domain-specific terminology for business profiles.

04
Global framework connection

Connect Hiroshima-based and internationally based operations to global AI governance frameworks — including the Hiroshima AI Process, NIST AI RMF, and ISO/IEC 42001.

Participation does not require

Exclusive adoption of any specific technology. Organizations retain full choice over their technical stack. Responsibility OS, ADIC, and the reference PoC implementation are provided as open reference — not as mandatory components.

Membership fees or financial contribution at this stage. The Council is in early formation and participation terms are being defined collaboratively.

Representing or endorsing the Hiroshima AI Process or any government body. The Council is independent of and not affiliated with any government authority.

Participation form and scope are flexible at this stage. Organizations may contribute through specification review, field validation, domain expertise, or formal membership. Contact us to discuss what makes sense for your situation.
03
Scope and independence

What the Council provides
and does not provide.

What the Council provides
◎A practical protocol specification for verifiable AI adoption — Hiroshima AI Assurance Protocol, currently Draft v0.1.
◎Shared vocabulary based on the Responsibility OS glossary, maintained consistently across the specification and field implementations.
◎Field validation PoC projects with participating organizations, beginning with the Logistics Profile.
◎Connection to global AI governance frameworks as a reference point for specification design.
◎Open reference implementations through GhostDrift Mathematical Institute (Responsibility OS, ADIC, PoC repositories).
What the Council does not provide
—Legal certification or compliance guarantees for AI systems or organizations.
—Government endorsement or representation of the Hiroshima AI Process.
—Certification marks or assumption of members' legal liability.
—Mandatory technology adoption — any specific platform, library, or vendor.
—Substitution for legal, regulatory, organizational governance, or risk management processes.
—A promise that every AI outcome can be controlled or that all accountability obligations are automatically satisfied.

Governance · 設立フェーズ

The Council is in its formation phase. Formal governance structure — decision-making process, participation tiers, working groups, and voting rights — is being developed collaboratively with founding members.

IP & Specification policy

The Hiroshima AI Assurance Protocol specification is developed under the Council's open governance. Reference implementations (Responsibility OS, ADIC) are available as open repositories. The Logistics Decision Packet technology is subject to a joint patent application by 前木秀光 and 株式会社オンザリンクス.

The relationship between the patent, the Protocol specification, and participant IP rights is currently being formalized. Prospective members with questions about IP, contribution licensing, or specification rights are encouraged to raise these directly in initial contact.

特許と仕様書の関係、参加者のIP権については現在整理中です。詳細はお問い合わせください。

Participation does not confer IP rights in the joint patent. The Council's specification is intended to remain openly referenceable independent of any single technology.
