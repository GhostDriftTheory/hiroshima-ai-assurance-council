Beacon
Cycle

From guiding principles to responsible action and verifiable review.

守るべき原則を、責任ある実行と検証可能な見直しへ。

Organizations cannot control every outcome. They can define which conditions must be satisfied before action, what evidence is required, who is responsible, and how decisions remain independently reviewable.

組織は、すべての結果を完全には制御できません。しかし、実行前に満たすべき条件、必要な証拠、責任主体、後から検証する方法を設計することはできます。

02 · What Is Beacon
A stable point of reference

変化の中で、判断基準を失わないために。

Definition

Beacon is the guiding purpose, principles, and boundaries an organization uses when determining whether an important decision or action is acceptable.Beaconとは、重要な判断や行動を認めてよいかを決める際に基準となる、守るべき目的・原則・越えてはならない境界です。

A Beacon is not a vision statement or motivational slogan. It is the stable reference point an organization can return to when assessing whether a specific action is permissible — regardless of how conditions change around it.

  • —Not a KPI or performance target
  • —Not a short-term achievement goal
  • —Not a request for personal belief or loyalty
  • —Not changed through ordinary operational improvement
On changing Beacon itself

A Beacon may require revision when it is found to be incorrect, when legal or societal responsibilities shift, or when it produces unintended consequences. Changing Beacon requires a separate fundamental review — outside the normal cycle — not routine redesign.Beacon自体の変更は、通常のサイクル外の根本審査によります。

03 · The Model
The Cycle

Six steps connecting principles
to verifiable action.

Beacon Cycle is an operational model that translates stable guiding principles into responsibility design, pre-execution assurance, authorized action, verifiable records, independent verification, and evidence-based redesign.Beacon Cycleは、守るべき原則を、責任設計、実行前保証、実行、証拠記録、独立検証、見直しへつなぐ運用モデルです。

Click or tap to enlarge
Record and version accountability-relevant information throughout the cycle — not only after execution.
全工程を通じて責任情報を記録・版管理する。実行後だけではありません。
Step 01
Responsibility Design
責任設計

Translate the Beacon into operating conditions, evidence requirements, responsibility boundaries, approval conditions, and exception handling.

Step 02
Pre-Execution Assurance
実行前保証

Before an operation changes real-world state, check required conditions, evidence, authority, approvals, policy version, pre-operation state, and expected scope of effect.

Step 03
Authorized Action
実行

Execute only the operation bound to a satisfied assurance result. If assurance conditions are not met, do not proceed.

Step 04
Verifiable Record
証拠記録

Bind the decision, conditions, evidence, approvals, unverified conditions, pre-operation state, and execution result so they can be reviewed or recalculated later.

Step 05
Independent Verification
独立検証

Confirm, from a standpoint independent of the executor, whether responsibility design, pre-execution assurance, execution, and records remained consistent.

Step 06
Review & Redesign
見直し

Use verification results to update conditions, responsibility boundaries, evidence requirements, approval processes, and verification methods.

04 · Step 02 in Detail
The Pre-Execution Gate

設計しただけでは、
実行は認められない。

Responsibility design does not authorize action by itself. The required assurance conditions must be satisfied at the time of execution.

責任設計が存在するだけでは、実行は認められません。実行時点で必要な保証条件が成立している必要があります。

✓
Conditions satisfied
Proceed to the authorized action.

条件成立 — 実行へ進む。

All required conditions, evidence, authority, and approvals are confirmed. The operation may proceed within its authorized scope.

!
Unverified or insufficient evidence
Hold the operation and request confirmation.

未確認・証拠不足 — 保留し、確認を求める。

Evidence is missing, ambiguous, or outside the acceptable window. The operation is held until confirmation is received.

✕
Conditions not satisfied
Do not execute the state-changing operation.

条件不成立 — 状態を変える操作を実行しない。

Required conditions are not met. The operation does not proceed. The result is recorded as a rejected assurance attempt.

05 · Context
How it relates to PDCA

Not a replacement
for PDCA.

Beacon Cycle does not supersede Plan-Do-Check-Act or claim alignment with ISO standards. It addresses a different question. PDCAを置き換えるものではなく、補完関係にあります。

Aspect PDCA Beacon Cycle
Main concern Improving performance and processes Whether important decisions and actions remain justifiable and verifiable
Sequence Plan → Do → Check → Act Responsibility Design → Pre-Execution Assurance → Authorized Action → Verifiable Record → Independent Verification → Review & Redesign
Evaluation focus Targets, gaps, and outcomes Conditions, evidence, responsibility, unresolved assumptions, and reviewability
Blocking mechanism Not a feature — PDCA improves after the fact Pre-execution gate: operations that fail assurance do not proceed
PDCA asks how an organization can improve its work. The Beacon Cycle additionally asks whether an important action was permitted under defined conditions and whether that decision remains independently verifiable.PDCAは業務をどのように改善するかを問います。Beacon Cycleはそれに加えて、重要な実行が定められた条件の下で認められたか、その判断を後から独立して確かめられるかを問います。
06 · Architecture
One operating model, three supporting layers

一つの運用モデルを支える
三つの基盤。

Beacon Cycle is the operational layer. Responsibility OS, HAAP, and ADIC each provide a distinct supporting function beneath it. They are not the same thing, and should not be used interchangeably.

Operational model
Beacon Cycle
Defines how an organization repeatedly designs responsibility conditions, assures execution before state changes, records decisions verifiably, verifies independently, and revises based on evidence.
Conceptual & formal foundation
Responsibility OS責任OS

Structures decisions, responsibility, evidence, unverified conditions, and state transitions together as a formal system. Provides the mathematical and conceptual basis for what the Beacon Cycle tracks.

Interoperability layer
Hiroshima AI Assurance Protocol広島AIアシュアランス・プロトコル

Defines how requirements, accountability-relevant information, evidence, verification outcomes, adoption states, and authorized transitions can be handled across organizations and systems.

Reference assurance implementation
ADICAdvanced Data Integrity by Ledger of Computation

Implements pre-execution checks, replayable evidence records, independent recalculation, integrity verification, and post-execution comparison. Reference implementation for the Protocol's verification mechanisms.

07 · Applications
Where it can apply

Any domain where conditions matter
before action is taken.

Beacon Cycle can apply wherever important decisions require verifiable conditions, responsible parties, and reviewable evidence. The following are illustrative — not exhaustive, and not presented as validated deployments.

Logistics
Pharmaceutical handoff

Before a pharmaceutical handoff, verify temperature evidence, custody records, authority, and acceptance conditions. If conditions are not satisfied, do not proceed with the handoff.

製薬物流の引き渡し前に、温度記録、保管証拠、権限、受取条件を確認できる領域で適用可能です。

Cybersecurity
Critical configuration changes

Before external data transmission, privilege escalation, or critical configuration changes, verify required evidence and approvals. Operations without sufficient assurance do not proceed.

外部データ送信、権限昇格、重要設定変更の前に、証拠と承認を確認できる領域で対象となり得ます。

AI Agents
Autonomous decision adoption

Before an autonomous agent selects a provider or changes business state, verify task requirements, accountability-relevant information, evidence, and authority. State changes without verified assurance do not proceed.

自律エージェントが業務状態を変える前に、要件、責任情報、証拠、権限を確認できる領域で適用できます。

The Beacon Cycle is not a certification scheme, a legal guarantee, an automated ethics score, or a promise that every outcome can be controlled. It is a structured operational model for making responsibility conditions, evidence, verification, and revision explicit.

Beacon Cycleは、認証制度、法的保証、倫理スコア、または全ての結果を制御できるという約束ではありません。責任条件、証拠、検証、見直しを明示的に扱うための運用モデルです。
