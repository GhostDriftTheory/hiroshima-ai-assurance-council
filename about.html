How We Operate

Public scope,
technical evidence,
member review.

The Council develops specifications, reference vocabulary, and implementation PoCs. Validated findings and future proposals are kept separate so the site does not overstate maturity.

01Reviewed public materials

Protocol materials and public claims should be reviewed by participating members before release.

02Open reference technology

Responsibility OS and ADIC are reference foundations. Participation does not require exclusive adoption of them.

03Formation stage

Formal membership terms, voting rights, working groups, and IP policy are being developed with founding members.

Scope Boundaries

What the Council
does not claim.

  • Does not represent Hiroshima Prefecture, Hiroshima City, the G7, or any government body.
  • Does not represent or act on behalf of the Hiroshima AI Process.
  • Does not provide legal certification, compliance guarantees, or safety endorsements.
  • Does not require adoption of any single vendor, platform, or technology.
  • Does not function as a sales channel for GhostDrift or ON THE LINKS.
  • Does not promise that every AI outcome can be controlled.
Why Hiroshima

Global agenda,
local implementation.

Hiroshima is internationally associated with the Hiroshima AI Process. The Council is independently operated and focuses on a narrower implementation question: how to translate trustworthy AI principles into verifiable operational conditions.
