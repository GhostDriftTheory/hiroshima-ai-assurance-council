About

From international agenda
to local implementation.

The Hiroshima AI Process (G7, 2023) established an international agenda for trustworthy AI. The Hiroshima AI Assurance Council is an independent, industry-led initiative that addresses the implementation gap — developing the protocol layer that makes verifiable AI adoption possible.

In logistics, pharmaceuticals, manufacturing, and public services, AI-assisted decisions are being adopted without the conditions, evidence, and accountability chain needed to verify them before or after the fact. The Council builds the common protocol to close that gap, from Hiroshima.

Hiroshima AI Assurance Protocol

Verified before action.
Preserved after.

Before an AI-assisted decision can change real-world state, required conditions, evidence, and authorizations must be verified against external sources. If verification is not satisfied, the state transition does not occur. Each AI-assisted decision is treated as an accountability-relevant information packet — holding the decision together with its inspection claims, evidence references, and adoption state.

01
Gate Before
State Change
Required conditions, evidence, and authorizations are verified before any state transition occurs. If verification is not satisfied, the transition does not occur.
02
Separate Claims
from Verification
Inspection claims travel inside the packet. Verification results come from external evidence — never from the packet itself. Adoption state is determined by verification results, not self-declaration.
03
Verify Against
External Evidence
Claims are re-calculated against independent evidence from separate sources — sensors, certifiers, systems. The packet cannot assert its own validity.
04
Record the Full
Accountability Chain
When a decision is adopted, the packet, evidence references, verification outcome, and adoption state are recorded together — preserving what was known, verified, and who was responsible.
05
Preserve as
Append-only Records
All records are stored as append-only entries with hash values, sequence numbers, and signatures. Nothing is overwritten; changes create new entries referencing prior ones.

What Must Remain Verifiable

後から確認できる状態に残すもの。

The Protocol defines which information must be preserved so responsible parties can review, audit, or verify an AI-assisted decision after adoption. The following elements — drawn from the Responsibility OS glossary — constitute accountability-relevant information when necessary for that review.

Metadata
メタデータ
When, where, and under what conditions the AI decision was made — version, configuration, and operational context.
Provenance
来歴
The origin and history of data, models, and processes that contributed to the decision — who produced what, under whose responsibility.
Audit Trail
監査証跡
A time-ordered record of who did what, when, in connection with the decision — including human review steps and intervention points.
Traceability
追跡可能性
The ability to trace a decision back to the agents, conditions, and data that produced it — linking result to origin independently.
Unverified Conditions
未確認条件
Assumptions or prerequisites not confirmed at decision time — what was not checked, and therefore cannot be guaranteed.
Accountability State
責任状態
The totality of who holds what responsibility at a given moment — who confirmed what, who was informed, and what remained unassigned.
When these elements are necessary to review, audit, or verify an AI-assisted decision after adoption, they constitute accountability-relevant information — the information that must not be lost.

Full vocabulary (provenance, traceability, audit trail, accountability state, noncommutativity, information loss, and more): see the Responsibility OS Glossary →
