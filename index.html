Hiroshima AI Assurance Protocol

Assured before action.
Verifiable afterward.

HAAP is a proposed assurance and interoperability layer connecting business rules with agent, tool, and transport protocols. It does not replace HTTP, gRPC, Agent2Agent (A2A), or Model Context Protocol (MCP). It defines how requirements, responsibility information, evidence, verification results, adoption states, and transition authorizations are linked and exchanged.

Pre-Execution Assurance
Before state change

Required conditions and evidence are evaluated before an AI-assisted decision is adopted as an organizational decision.

Claim–Evidence Separation
Claims are not verification

Inspection claims are evaluated against evidence independent of the claim, under trust requirements defined by the applicable profile.

Verifiable Record
Requirements, evidence references, verification results, adoption states, transition authorizations, and responsibility information are integrity-bound and preserved for later review, independent verification, and replay.
