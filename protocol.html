HAAP / Non-Normative Overview / Discussion Draft v0.1

Hiroshima AI Assurance Protocol
in one sentence.

HAAP lets organizations adopt an AI-assisted decision only after defined conditions are evaluated against profile-defined evidence. It then preserves the requirement, evidence, verification result, adoption state, and transition authorization as a replayable assurance record.

AI判断を採用する前に、事前に定めた条件を証拠に照らして検証し、要求・証拠・検証結果・採用状態・状態遷移許可を再検証可能な記録として残すための共通レイヤーです。

3-Line Summary

The protocol defines
what must travel together.

Task Requirement
What must be true for this operation?

A Task Requirements Packet binds the target operation, applicable profile and ruleset versions, evidence requirements, thresholds, authority, evaluation time, validity window, and permitted tolerances.

Responsibility Information
What evidence and responsibility information are available?

Accountability-Relevant Information Packets link claims, evidence references, responsible actors, authority, and unverified conditions for the relevant operation.

Verification and Authorization
What state transition is permitted?

The verifier records a verification outcome, adoption state, and transition authorization for the specified operation.

Position

A proposed layer for
verifiable adoption.

Transport, agent, and tool protocols move messages and connect capabilities. HAAP addresses a different question: can this AI-assisted decision be adopted as an organizational decision with conditions evaluated against profile-defined evidence?

01Agent2Agent (A2A) / Model Context Protocol (MCP)

Useful for agent and tool interaction. They do not by themselves define adoption conditions or accountability records.

02HTTP / gRPC

Useful for transport. They do not define evidence semantics, verification outcomes, or transition authorization.

03Business profiles

Contain reusable domain rules, while Task Requirements Packets bind those rules to a specific operation, evaluation time, and evidence set.

Protocol Records and Messages

Nine proposed semantic categories,
five functions tested in Phase 1.

This discussion draft describes protocol semantics, not a normative wire specification. JSON Schema, required fields, signing algorithms, trust anchors, error handling, and interoperability requirements remain under development.

"PoC-tested" means that the function was implemented and exercised against documented Phase 1 test scenarios. It does not mean independent certification, production validation, regulatory approval, or multi-implementation interoperability.

State Separation

Do not collapse
verification, adoption, and authorization.

Verification outcome: SATISFIED, UNSATISFIED, or INDETERMINATE.

Adoption state: ADOPTABLE, CONDITIONALLY_ADOPTABLE, NOT_ADOPTABLE, or PENDING_EVIDENCE.

Transition authorization: AUTHORIZED, REQUIRES_REVIEW, or NOT_AUTHORIZED.

Responsibility information is preserved inside the accountability-relevant information packet: who confirmed what, who retains responsibility, which conditions remain unverified, and which state transition the record concerns.

AUTHORIZED is a protocol-level state for a specified operation. It does not create legal, regulatory, contractual, safety, or organizational authority that did not otherwise exist.

Phase 1 PoC

Logistics Profile:
PoC-tested scope.

Phase 1 implemented and tested core verification and transition-authorization functions for pharmaceutical cold-chain logistics in Hiroshima. It is not yet a proof of the full protocol, production deployment, or regulatory compliance.

  • Ruleset binding at evaluation time.
  • Evidence field inspection and gap detection.
  • Independent temperature metric recalculation.
  • Logistics-profile disposition generation: RELEASE, HOLD, QA_REVIEW, or REJECT.
  • Sensor authenticity not covered.
  • Production integration not covered.
  • Cross-company API exchange not covered.
  • Multi-implementation interoperability not covered.

RELEASE, HOLD, QA_REVIEW, and REJECT are domain-specific dispositions used by the Logistics Profile. They are not replacements for HAAP's domain-independent verification outcomes, adoption states, or transition-authorization states.
